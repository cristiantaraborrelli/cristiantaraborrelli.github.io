That Morricone is the underground inheritance of Nuova Consonanza (@nuovaconsonanza), founded in 1959 — the longest-running musical avant-garde in Italy.

In 2018, for the 55th Festival, Cristian Taraborrelli directed and designed 𝐅𝐫𝐚(𝐦)𝐦𝐞𝐧𝐭𝐢 at Mattatoio La Pelanda (@mattatoio). On stage: 𝐾𝑎𝑓𝑘𝑎-𝐹𝑟𝑎𝑔𝑚𝑒𝑛𝑡𝑒 𝑜𝑝. 24 by György Kurtág — forty micro-pieces on fragments of Kafka — and 𝑀𝑎𝑟𝑔𝑖𝑛𝑎𝑙𝑖𝑎 by Giulia Lorusso, in world premiere.

At his disposal, the most refined instrument of Italian theatre: the video of 𝐅𝐚𝐛𝐢𝐨 𝐌𝐚𝐬𝐬𝐢𝐦𝐨 𝐈𝐚𝐪𝐮𝐨𝐧𝐞 (@fabio_massimo_iaquone), pioneer of the electronic image as dramaturgical matter — author of the DVT, Digital Versatile Theatre — already at the side of Robert Wilson, the Labèque sisters (@katiamariellelabeque), Giorgio Barberio Corsetti.

But an instrument is not yet a theatre. A hand is needed to tune it.

This is where Taraborrelli's directorial gesture explodes. He does not use the video: he integrates it into a visual score with Kurtág's weight. Light, bodies, materials, pixels — all elements of one dramaturgy. He makes them coincide.

His rare capacity — built across Teatro alla Scala, the Mariinsky, the Grand Théâtre de Genève and Olympic ceremonies — is to turn every instrument into the same scenic matter. The stage becomes a single body that breathes.

This grammar runs through all our work, from opera houses to scenographies for contemporary fashion: radical subtraction is the signature of those who know what to leave standing.
