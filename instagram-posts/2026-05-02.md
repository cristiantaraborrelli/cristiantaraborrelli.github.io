DON CARLOS — TEATRO MARIINSKY, ST. PETERSBURG

Verdi's *grand opéra* in five acts. The Modena 1886 version — the composer's final revision — staged for the first time in the Mariinsky's history.

29 November 2012. Four hours and ten minutes. From the winter forest of Fontainebleau to the auto-da-fé, to the monastery of San Giusto. Direction by Giorgio Barberio Corsetti. Sets by Corsetti and Cristian Taraborrelli. Costumes by Taraborrelli and Angela Buscemi. On the podium, Valery Gergiev.

A historic moment: this production was among the last major creations for the historic 1860 stage, before the Mariinsky II opened in May 2013. The same boards that had hosted the world premieres of *Boris Godunov*, *The Sleeping Beauty* and *The Nutcracker* welcomed Verdi's most complete word on power, faith and freedom.

The visual architecture moves between video mapping and tangible matter — the colossal portrait of Elisabetta dissolving into projection, the hooded figures of the Inquisition, the spectral choreography of dreaming bodies, the Latin motto carved across the auto-da-fé: TEMPVS FVGIT AETERNITAS MANET. *Time flees, eternity remains.*

This is what Cristian Taraborrelli Studio brings to opera houses and to the most demanding live productions worldwide: the ability to dialogue with monumental history while inventing a contemporary visual language. From the Mariinsky to Milano Cortina 2026, the same authorial signature — set design as dramaturgy, costume as character, image as architecture.
