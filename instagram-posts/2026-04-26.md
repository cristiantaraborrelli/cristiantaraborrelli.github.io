SANGEET — VILLA GASTEL, LAKE COMO

A 12th-century Benedictine convent. A Visconti residence where Federico Fellini once walked. For three nights in April 2024, Villa Gastel became an open-air theatre for one of the most ambitious private weddings ever staged on Lake Como.

Artistic direction and staging by @cristiantaraborrelli.director, in collaboration with @samit.garg and @efactorexperiences.

Every surface of the villa was rewritten in light. The immersive projections by @fabio_massimo_iaquone turned historic stone into living canvas; the choreography by @thomassignorelli moved in perfect sync with videomapping that embraced every gesture. Angela Buscemi's 1920s-inspired costumes — fine fabrics, intricate embroidery — turned each performer into a tableau vivant. @vincenzodascaniostudio composed a floral bridge worthy of an operatic finale.

The bride and groom arrived in a golden rain of confetti, framed by six-metre flames rising into the night sky, with laser mapping and lighting design by Massimo Tomasino. Soprano @serena_gamberoni and violinist @stellamanfrediviolinist filled the spaces with rare emotion. @stefaniaventura_artist — Cyr wheel and Lollipop — added the gravity-defying counterpoint. Tangueros @pablovalentinmoyano and @ambra_amari brought the Argentine pulse, weaving milonga into the score.

This is what private events become when treated as theatre rather than décor: dramaturgy, scenography, costume and music as a single score. The same grammar we apply to opera houses and ceremonies, scaled to the intimacy of a destination wedding or a couture show.
