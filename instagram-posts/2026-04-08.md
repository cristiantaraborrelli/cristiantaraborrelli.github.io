𝑨𝒏𝒄𝒊𝒆𝒏𝒕 𝑮𝒓𝒆𝒆𝒄𝒆 𝒔𝒆𝒆𝒏 𝒕𝒉𝒓𝒐𝒖𝒈𝒉 𝑪𝒊𝒏𝒆𝒄𝒊𝒕𝒕𝒂̀.

For Offenbach's La Belle Hélène at the Théâtre du Châtelet in Paris — on stage from 2 to 22 June 2015 — Cristian Taraborrelli created approximately 80 stage costumes. The opera uses the myth of Paris and Helen as a pretext to dismantle Napoleonic France: three acts of political satire dressed as mythological comedy. The visual language required was at once archaic and cinematic — classical antiquity filtered through the imagery of the great Italian epic films of the 1950s and '60s: the powerful stylisation that gave the ancient world its most recognisable modern face.

𝑨 𝒇𝒊𝒈𝒖𝒓𝒊𝒏𝒐 𝒊𝒔 𝒏𝒐𝒕 𝒂𝒏 𝒊𝒍𝒍𝒖𝒔𝒕𝒓𝒂𝒕𝒊𝒐𝒏 — 𝒊𝒕 𝒊𝒔 𝒕𝒉𝒆 𝒇𝒊𝒓𝒔𝒕 𝒄𝒓𝒆𝒂𝒕𝒊𝒗𝒆 𝒂𝒄𝒕 𝒐𝒇 𝒂 𝒄𝒐𝒔𝒕𝒖𝒎𝒆.

For this production, 𝗔𝗻𝗴𝗲𝗹𝗮 𝗕𝘂𝘀𝗰𝗲𝗺𝗶 — the Studio's costume sketch designer — translated the vision into drawing: a series of models reinterpreting historical costume with an echo of haute couture, combining technical precision and aesthetic sensibility to define the visual character of the entire production.

With 𝗚𝗮𝗲̈𝗹𝗹𝗲 𝗔𝗿𝗾𝘂𝗲𝘇 as Hélène, Jesús León as Pâris, Gilles Ragon as Ménélas, Jean-Philippe Lafont as Calchas. Conducted by Lorenzo Viotti. Direction, set design and video by 𝗚𝗶𝗼𝗿𝗴𝗶𝗼 𝗕𝗮𝗿𝗯𝗲𝗿𝗶𝗼 𝗖𝗼𝗿𝘀𝗲𝘁𝘁𝗶 and 𝗣𝗶𝗲𝗿𝗿𝗶𝗰𝗸 𝗦𝗼𝗿𝗶𝗻.

Premio Franco Abbiati 2004 & 2006 · Prix du Syndicat de la Critique 2009 · Molière nomination.
