A forest grew inside a factory in Turin.

For **Iveco Group's "Connecting the Dots" Gala Dinner** at the Industrial Village, Cristian Taraborrelli signed a living set design: real, full-grown trees arranged in a clearing inside the 74.000 sqm exhibition hall. Circular mirrors on the floor multiplied the canopies downward; violet, pink and amber laser beams drew a geometric sky in continuous motion above the leaves.

At the heart of the clearing, a grand piano and two violinists in red gowns. Pianissimo: the canopies turned deep blue. Crescendo: magenta erupted through the foliage. Guests dined among the trees — no separation between stage and audience.

The radical gesture was not technological. It was ethical. **Every single tree was replanted in the venue park after the event.** Zero scenic waste. A literal translation of Iveco Group's industrial narrative — eight brands "connecting their dots" toward a shared trajectory — into matter that keeps growing once the lights go down.

Laser mapping on irregular natural surfaces, not the customary LED wall. Living scenography, not a backdrop. A benchmark cited by the corporate events trade press as a model for eco-friendly large-scale productions.

Produced by **Filmmaster Events** — among the world's most awarded live-communication agencies, more than eleven BEA Italia and BEA World trophies.

Same architecture — trees, mirrors, treetop mapping, live chamber music — translates directly into runway scenography. A fashion show inside a forest is one rehearsal away.
