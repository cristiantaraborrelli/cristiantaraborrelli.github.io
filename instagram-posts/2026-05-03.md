LA JURA — TEATRO LIRICO DI CAGLIARI, 2015

A work that waited fifty-six years for its own redress.

Gavino Gabriel — pioneer of Italian ethnomusicology, frequenter of the D'Annunzio and Prezzolini circle, first director of the Discoteca di Stato — writes La Jura from 1905 to 1959. He rewrites it, abandons it, returns to it, deposits it in the archive of the Accademia Popolare Gallurese of Tempio Pausania like a private manuscript. In 1959, during rehearsals for the Cagliari revival, the conductor Dante Ullu tears pages from the score. Wounded, Gabriel rewrites the entire opera in a single summer. The version of 18 September 1959 is not a variant: it is a response to a betrayal. When he dies in 1980, that version has never been performed.

The 2015 staging at the @teatroliricodicagliari is its first integral performance, in the critical edition by Susanna Pasticci, author of the essay «Sperimentare la tradizione». Pasticci does not retrieve: she establishes. She is the architect of the project — without her philological work La Jura would have remained a phantom.

I signed, for the first time, direction, sets and costumes together. La Jura cannot bear division. It refuses the verismo melodrama that surrounds it — Mascagni, Leoncavallo — and summons an elsewhere: an ethnographic-modernist device, five tableaux not of action but of environment — sagra, conche, fontana, pricunta, zidda — where the characters do not act, they are acted upon by the places. A single mind was needed to hold this unity together.

Angela Buscemi's costumes — t'agus, fazzolettoni, Gallurese embroideries — are philology worn on the body. They do not cite, they refound. The lighting of Guido Levi, no longer with us, sculpted the stage with the discipline of one who treats light as thought. The privilege was mine. The video-scenography of Fabio Massimo Iaquone (@fabio_massimo_iaquone) and Luca Attilii (@luca_attilii) — a partnership of twenty years — found here its purest form: the closing loom of nine and a half kilometres of elastic threads, an explicit citation of Maria Lai, with the women stretching the warp like the Fates of Greek tragedy.

Bruno Di Marino (@b.dima1966) read the staging as «an example of intermedial re-reading of a musical text». His essay for the programme bears the title «Nel cuore del Novecento», and closes with the line that has defined for me the meaning of the entire operation:

«We have finally entered the heart of the twentieth century.»

On the podium Sandro Sanna; choreography Antonella Agati; chorus master Gaetano Mastroiaco; tàsgia chant by the Accademia Popolare Gallurese «Gavino Gabriel»; set construction Silvano Santinelli. On stage @rubenspelizzari, @gtalamo80, @paolettamarrocu, Caruso, Lentini, Pierpaoli, Ebau.

La Jura was not ours. It had belonged to Gabriel, to the archive, to Sardinia. For two weeks of November we held it in custody.
