LA JURA — TEATRO LIRICO DI CAGLIARI, 2015

A tribute, a costume, a piece of light.

Five tableaux of Gallurese life: sagra, conche, fontana, pricunta, zidda. Five stations of an impossible love — the shepherd-poet Cicciottu Jacòni and Anna, whose hand her father promises to the wealthy shepherd Burédda. Between them, the *jura* — the ancient Sardinian ordeal oath that binds destinies and seals the catastrophe.

Gavino Gabriel (1881–1980) worked on this score for over half a century, from the first draft in 1905 to the definitive five-act version completed in 1959. The 2015 staging at the Teatro Lirico di Cagliari was the world premiere of the complete work — and Cristian Taraborrelli's first opera lirica signed as director, designer and costumier together.

★ IN MEMORIAM — GUIDO LEVI
This production owes its breath to a master who is no longer with us. Guido Levi — luminous architect, lighting designer of singular intelligence — carved the stage into a Sardinia of dust and dust-of-stars, of sirocco and shadow. His silence is what the spectator carries home.

★ ANGELA BUSCEMI — Costumes
A peerless figurinista. She brought the world of Sardinian tradition back onto the stage with painterly precision: t'agus, fazzolettoni, embroideries, the geometries of pastoral memory recomposed as living archive — never folkloric quotation, never decoration.

«The video-scenic device heightens the dreamlike and visionary atmosphere not only of this scene, but of the entire production.»
— Bruno Di Marino, *Nel cuore del Novecento*, Programma di Sala, Teatro Lirico di Cagliari, 2015

Direction, sets and costumes by Cristian Taraborrelli. Conducting by Sandro Sanna. Choreography by Antonella Agati. Video-scenography by Fabio Massimo Iaquone. Tàsgia chants by the Accademia Popolare Gallurese «Gavino Gabriel». Critical edition by Susanna Pasticci.
