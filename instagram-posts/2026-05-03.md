LA JURA — TEATRO LIRICO DI CAGLIARI, 2015

Not just any memory. One of the most beautiful works I have ever signed.

Five tableaux of Gallurese life — sagra, conche, fontana, pricunta, zidda — composed by Gavino Gabriel across half a century of writing, from 1905 to 1959. The complete version waited until 2015 to reach the stage in its entirety. That year, at the Teatro Lirico di Cagliari, I signed my first opera lirica as director, set designer and costumier together. Surrounded by a team I will remember for the rest of my life.

In the programme of that opening, Bruno Di Marino wrote:

«The video-scenic device heightens the dreamlike and visionary atmosphere not only of this scene, but of the entire production.»

That atmosphere was not the work of one element. It was born from the continuous dialogue of every pair of hands:

★ SANDRO SANNA — the baton that brought a forgotten score back to life, restoring the original breath of Gabriel's music.

★ ANGELA BUSCEMI — peerless figurinista: t'agus, fazzolettoni, Gallurese embroideries, costumes that did not cite folklore but recomposed its living archive — never decoration, always memory wearable on the body.

★ GUIDO LEVI — lighting design. He left us recently. A silent genius who treated light as thought, never as decoration. Working with him was one of the great experiences of my career — a sensibility of singular precision, an absolute ear for visual dramaturgy. His departure leaves a stage that is dimmer, and the duty to keep his lesson visible.

★ FABIO MASSIMO IAQUONE and LUCA ATTILII — video-scenography: nine and a half kilometres of elastic threads turned into a cosmic loom of the Fates.

★ ANTONELLA AGATI — choreography that made a folk chorus dance with the discipline of an opera company.

★ GAETANO MASTROIACO and the ACCADEMIA POPOLARE GALLURESE «Gavino Gabriel» — the tàsgia chant brought for the first time onto the operatic stage in its full living form.

★ SILVANO SANTINELLI — set construction. SUSANNA PASTICCI — critical edition for the Fondazione Gavino Gabriel.

Cast: Pelizzari/Talamo (Cicciottu), Marrocu/Caruso (Anna), Lentini, Pierpaoli, Ebau, and the whole company.

I believed then — and I believe today even more — that we made something unforgettable. Not for the set design. Not for the costumes. Not for the direction. But because that day, in that theatre, twenty-two pairs of hands kept alive an opera that had been forgotten, and gave it back to the world.

A jura. Five tableaux of Gallura. Half a century of writing. And a team that made theatre.
