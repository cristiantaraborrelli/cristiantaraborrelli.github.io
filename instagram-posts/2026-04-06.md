Pagliacci — AR Breaks into Opera.
Teatro Carlo Felice, Genova — 2021.

The question was: what if the inner world of Canio — his visions, his jealousy, his collapse into violence — could become *visible*?

For the world premiere of 3D augmented reality in opera, I transformed the Carlo Felice stage into something between a theatre and a live television studio. Two chroma key backdrops (24×12 m and 20×10 m), a green-screen floor, five cameras processing the scene in real time — and an entirely virtual scenography reconstructed behind the performers, adapting dynamically to every camera angle.

The singers moved in physical space. The audience lived a dual reality: bodies on a real stage floor, the augmented world unfolding above on a large LED screen. Rai 5 broadcast the production live; it streams today on Rai Play.

AR was not a technological addition. It was a dramaturgical tool — the only medium capable of rendering what Leoncavallo actually wrote: a man who can no longer tell the stage from life.

Angela Buscemi's costumes carried the commedia dell'arte tradition into the present with precision and emotional depth. Cast: Fabio Sartori as Canio, Serena Gamberoni as Nedda, Sebastian Catana as Tonio. Video design: Luca Attilii. Technical direction: Luciano Novelli. Production: Teatro Carlo Felice / Rai Cultura.

A production that redefined what opera can be — technically, emotionally, dramatically.
