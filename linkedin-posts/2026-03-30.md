## Progetto: Italian Souvenir — Paralympic Closing Ceremony, Milano Cortina 2026
## Account: Cristian Taraborrelli

---

There is a specific kind of pressure in a closing ceremony: everything has already happened. The Games are over. What remains is the space between the last competition and the final image the world will carry home.

*Italian Souvenir* — the Closing Ceremony of the Milano Cortina 2026 Paralympic Winter Games — was designed around that question. My role covered the full arc: Content Supervisor, Show Caller, and Scenic Design. I shaped the show's dramaturgy and directed its live execution in real time from the Cortina Curling Olympic Stadium on March 15, 2026, broadcast worldwide by the IPC.

The scenic contribution I'm most proud of is the *Boule de Neige* — a monumental luminous sphere enclosing the Duomo di Milano and the Dolomites within a single image. When Sofia, the young protagonist, blew out the Paralympic flame, the arena held its breath. That pause — a few seconds of shared silence between a child and a sphere of light — was the ceremony's emotional fulcrum.

Ceremonies at this scale are not spectacles. They are architectural acts: you are building a shared memory that will persist long after the broadcast ends.
