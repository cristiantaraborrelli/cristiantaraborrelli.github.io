## Tema: La Jura (Cagliari 2015) — Critical-biographical register
## Versione: v4 (post 5 fix critici allineati alla regola "biografia d'arte")

A work that waited fifty-six years for its own redress.

Gavino Gabriel — pioneer of Italian ethnomusicology, first director of the Discoteca di Stato — writes *La Jura* from 1905 to 1959. He rewrites it, abandons it, returns to it. In 1959, during rehearsals at Cagliari, the conductor Dante Ullu tears pages from the score; Gabriel, wounded, rewrites the entire opera in a single summer. The version of 18 September 1959 is not a variant: it is a response to a betrayal. When he dies in 1980, that version has never been performed.

The 2015 staging at the Teatro Lirico di Cagliari gives the work its first integral performance, in the critical edition by **Susanna Pasticci**, author of the essay *Sperimentare la tradizione*. Pasticci does not retrieve: she establishes.

I signed for the first time direction, sets and costumes together. *La Jura* cannot bear division. It refuses the verismo melodrama that surrounds it and summons an elsewhere — an ethnographic-modernist device where characters do not act, they are acted upon by the places.

Beside me: **Angela Buscemi**, whose costumes did not cite Gallurese tradition but refounded it on stage; **Guido Levi**, lighting designer no longer with us, who treated light as thought — the privilege was mine; **Fabio Massimo Iaquone** and **Luca Attilii**, video-scenography in a partnership of twenty years; **Sandro Sanna** on the podium; **Antonella Agati** on choreography; the **Accademia Popolare Gallurese** for the *tàsgia* chant.

Bruno Di Marino, in his essay for the programme — *Nel cuore del Novecento* — read the production as «an intermedial re-reading of a musical text». He closed with the line that has defined for me the meaning of the entire operation:

«We have finally entered the heart of the twentieth century.»

*La Jura* was not ours. It had belonged to Gabriel, to the archive, to Sardinia. For two weeks of November we held it in custody.

In memoriam, Guido.
