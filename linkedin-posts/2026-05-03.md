## Tema: La Jura (Cagliari 2015) — In memoriam Guido Levi + tributo Angela Buscemi

---

**Light is a memory.**

For *La Jura* at the Teatro Lirico di Cagliari (2015) we worked alongside **Guido Levi** — luminous architect, lighting designer of rare intelligence — who left us too soon. His cuts of light carved the Sardinian stage into something close to memory itself: dust and dust-of-stars, sirocco and shadow, the silence after the *cunto*.

A line from **Bruno Di Marino**, in the programme of that opening, has stayed with me: *«The video-scenic device heightens the dreamlike and visionary atmosphere not only of this scene, but of the entire production.»* It is true. Light, costume and projection were one breathing organism — and Guido was its eyes.

Beside him, **Angela Buscemi** — figurinista of the rarest sensibility — recomposed the visual archive of Gallurese tradition into wearable poetry: *t'agus, fazzolettoni*, embroideries that carried memory without ever lapsing into folklore.

The five tableaux of Gavino Gabriel's opera — composed across half a century, from 1905 to 1959 — found in 2015 their first integral staging at the Teatro Lirico di Cagliari, in the critical edition by Susanna Pasticci. For me it was also a passage: my first opera lirica fully signed as director.

What I carry from that work, ten years on, is the conviction that an opera is not a text or a score in isolation: it is the sum of the minds that meet in a rehearsal room, and of the geniuses who depart while still leaving every cut of light at its right place.

In memoriam, Guido.
