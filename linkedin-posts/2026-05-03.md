## Tema: La Jura (Cagliari 2015) — A homage to the team. In memoriam Guido Levi.

---

A homage to the team of *La Jura*.

«The video-scenic device heightens the dreamlike and visionary atmosphere not only of this scene, but of the entire production.» So **Bruno Di Marino** wrote in the programme of the Teatro Lirico di Cagliari, 2015.

That sentence, today, is the key to the most important work I have ever signed as director — my first complete opera lirica, direction, set and costumes together. But that dreamlike and visionary atmosphere was not mine. It was *ours*.

It was the baton of **Sandro Sanna**, restoring breath to **Gavino Gabriel**'s score after half a century of silence. It was the exceptional costumes of **Angela Buscemi**, who recomposed on stage the world of Sardinian tradition — t'agus, fazzolettoni, embroideries — as living archive, never folklore. It was the cuts of light of **Guido Levi**, who recently left us, a silent genius I had the privilege to watch at work: his sensibility treated light as thought, and every shadow had a reason for being there. It was the video-scenography of **Fabio Massimo Iaquone** and **Luca Attilii**, the choreographies of **Antonella Agati**, the tàsgia chant directed by **Gaetano Mastroiaco** with the **Accademia Popolare Gallurese**, the critical edition of **Susanna Pasticci**, the construction by **Silvano Santinelli**, and the voices of our singers.

Ten years on, I take two things from that work: the conviction that an opera is never signed alone, and that certain experiences — when you really meet artists — remain unforgettable. Cagliari was one of those.

In memoriam, Guido. With gratitude to all.
