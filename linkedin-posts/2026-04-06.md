## Progetto/Servizio: Pagliacci AR — Teatro Carlo Felice, Genova 2021
## Account: Cristian Taraborrelli

---

In 2021, Teatro Carlo Felice asked a practical question: how do you stage Leoncavallo's Pagliacci when a live television broadcast is at the centre of the production? My answer was to turn the opera house into a studio — and, in doing so, to attempt something that had never been done in the history of opera: integrate 3D augmented reality not as spectacle, but as dramaturgy.

The technical setup was substantial. Two chroma key backdrops covering the full depth of the stage, five cameras processing performers' silhouettes in real time, a virtual scenography that shifted dynamically with every camera angle. Broadcast live on Rai 5, now on Rai Play. But the decision that mattered most was conceptual.

AR was chosen because Pagliacci is, at its core, a story about the failure of the boundary between imagination and reality. Canio's visions needed to be visible. They needed to inhabit the same space as the singers. Set design has always been asked to do exactly this — make interior states tangible. The technology changed; the question remained the same.

For agencies and producers working at the intersection of live performance and broadcast — institutional events, fashion presentations, hybrid formats that require both stage presence and media-ready visual output — this kind of integration is increasingly the standard to reach.

The production is available on Rai Play.
