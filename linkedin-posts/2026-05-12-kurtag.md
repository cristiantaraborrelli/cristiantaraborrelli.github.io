# LinkedIn Post — 2026-05-12 (secondo post, teatro)
## Tipo: Portfolio
## Progetto: Fra(m)menti — Kurtág & Lorusso, Mattatoio La Pelanda Roma 2018
## Account: Cristian Taraborrelli

---

There is a moment in every project when you have to decide what to take away.

For **Fra(m)menti** — staged at Mattatoio La Pelanda in Rome for the 55th Festival di Nuova Consonanza — that moment came early. The work was built on **György Kurtág's *Kafka-Fragmente op. 24*** and on **Giulia Lorusso's *Marginalia***, written for the same forces and premiered that night. Kurtág's pieces sometimes last only a few seconds. Forty micro-works on fragments of Kafka. The most radical statement music has made in the last forty years on density per second.

A scenography that tried to *frame* that material would have killed it.

So I built a device, not a set. A few mobile elements, poor materials treated as precious, blades of light cutting through the black. A soprano and a violinist who were not surrounded by scenography but who *became* it. Video — by Fabio Massimo Iaquone — as amplification, never illustration. And a workshop, parallel to the rehearsals, that brought a group of young performers on stage with their smartphones treated as prostheses of the body: luminous fragments, exactly as Kurtág treats sound.

What I learned on that production has shaped every project since, including the very different scale of corporate scenography and live ceremonies: the audience does not need to be told what to feel. They need a space precise enough to let attention return.

Subtraction as a method is a luxury rarely permitted in tender briefs. But it is the most expensive thing a director can offer — and the only one that lasts beyond the evening.
