## Progetto: Iveco — Connecting the Dots, Industrial Village Turin 2023
## Account: Cristian Taraborrelli

---

The most radical decision in corporate scenography is rarely about technology. It is about what stays.

For **Iveco Group's "Connecting the Dots" Gala Dinner** at the Industrial Village in Turin, we built a forest inside a factory. Real, full-grown trees composed a clearing across 74.000 square metres of exhibition hall. Circular mirrors multiplied the canopies on the floor; suspended above, laser beams drew a moving geometric sky. Two violinists and a pianist played at the heart of the composition — the mapping reacting in real time to the dynamics of the music.

The technical brief was clear: translate the spin-off of Iveco Group from CNH Industrial — eight brands learning to tell a single story — into a scenographic gesture. We chose to make every node of that story literal. Trees as dots. Light as the connection between them.

The non-negotiable was sustainability. Every tree present that evening was replanted in the venue park after the show. No element was discarded. The set kept growing.

This is the part that interests me as a director: a corporate audience expecting LED walls left the dinner having watched living matter. The hierarchy between technology and nature inverted for one night, on a strict industrial brief. That inversion is what I think will increasingly define the next decade of live events — including fashion productions, where runway design is asking the same question of its scenographies.
