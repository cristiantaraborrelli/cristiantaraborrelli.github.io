𝗘𝗶𝗴𝗵𝘁𝘆 𝗰𝗼𝘀𝘁𝘂𝗺𝗲𝘀 𝗳𝗼𝗿 𝗮𝗻 𝗼𝗽𝗲𝗿𝗮 𝘁𝗵𝗮𝘁 𝗶𝘀, 𝗮𝘁 𝗶𝘁𝘀 𝗰𝗼𝗿𝗲, 𝗮𝗯𝗼𝘂𝘁 𝗮𝗽𝗽𝗲𝗮𝗿𝗮𝗻𝗰𝗲𝘀.

Offenbach's 𝘓𝘢 𝘉𝘦𝘭𝘭𝘦 𝘏é𝘭è𝘯𝘦 at the Théâtre du Châtelet in Paris — 2–22 June 2015, directed by Giorgio Barberio Corsetti and Pierrick Sorin, conducted by Lorenzo Viotti — was one of the most visually ambitious productions in my work in music theatre. Approximately 80 costumes inspired by ancient Greece, reinterpreted through the aesthetic of the great Italian epic cinema of the 1950s and '60s: Cinecittà as a cultural filter, not a nostalgic reference.

🎭 With Gaëlle Arquez as Hélène, Jesús León as Pâris, Gilles Ragon as Ménélas, Jean-Philippe Lafont as Calchas.

𝗧𝗵𝗲 𝗳𝗶𝗴𝘂𝗿𝗶𝗻𝗼 𝗶𝘀 𝗻𝗼𝘁 𝗮𝗻 𝗶𝗹𝗹𝘂𝘀𝘁𝗿𝗮𝘁𝗶𝗼𝗻 — 𝗶𝘁 𝗶𝘀 𝘁𝗵𝗲 𝗳𝗶𝗿𝘀𝘁 𝗰𝗿𝗲𝗮𝘁𝗶𝘃𝗲 𝗮𝗰𝘁 𝗼𝗳 𝗮 𝗰𝗼𝘀𝘁𝘂𝗺𝗲.

At the centre of this work is the collaboration with Angela Buscemi, the Studio's costume sketch designer. The 𝘧𝘪𝘨𝘶𝘳𝘪𝘯𝘰 is the phase in which the scenic vision becomes a buildable project — drama becomes cut, proportion, fabric. For this production, Angela Buscemi developed a series of models reinterpreting historical costume with an echo of haute couture, combining philological rigour and expressive freedom, and ensuring the visual coherence that eighty different characters make particularly complex to achieve.

❝ Humouristic, artful and with a feeling of limitless possibilities. Pure eye candy and a generous homage to the mysteries and extravagances of music theater. ❞
