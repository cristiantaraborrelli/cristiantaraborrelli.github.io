## Progetto: Sangeet — Villa Gastel, Lake Como 2024

---

There is a difference between decorating a venue and staging it.

Sangeet at Villa Gastel — a private wedding show on Lake Como, April 2024 — was approached as theatre. Not as floral arrangement, not as event design. Theatre. With a dramaturgy, a score, a scenography, and a cast.

The site itself imposed it: a 12th-century Benedictine convent, later residence of the Visconti di Modrone, a place where Federico Fellini sought inspiration. A venue of that density does not accept ornament. It demands narrative.

Together with the production of Samit Garg / E-Factor Experiences, the studio coordinated a single creative score: Fabio Massimo Iaquone's immersive projections rewriting the historic facades; Thomas Signorelli's choreography moving in synchrony with videomapping; Angela Buscemi's 1920s-inspired costumes constructed as tableaux vivants; Vincenzo Dascanio's floral architecture forming the bridge of the grand entrance, framed by six-metre flames and Massimo Tomasino's laser mapping. Soprano Serena Gamberoni and violinist Stella Manfredi held the intimate register, while tangueros Pablo Valentin Moyano and Ambra Amari brought the Argentine pulse — milonga as the silent thread connecting East, West and the Italian lake.

The lesson, repeatable across luxury events, fashion shows and institutional ceremonies: when artistic direction governs every layer — scenography, costume, light, music, choreography — a private event acquires the gravity of a stage work.

This is the language Cristian Taraborrelli Studio brings to opera houses, Olympic ceremonies, and to the brands that want their events to carry the same weight.
