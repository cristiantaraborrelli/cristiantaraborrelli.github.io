L'Opera Illumina il Natale

Teatro dell'Opera di Roma · Teatro Costanzi

Client
Teatro dell'Opera di Roma

Role
Artistic Director & Director

Location
Piazza Beniamino Gigli, Roma

Year
2017

L'Opera Illumina il Natale is a spectacular 3D videomapping show that illuminated the facade of Teatro Costanzi during the Christmas season. Conceived and directed by Cristian Taraborrelli, the project blends opera, architectural projection mapping, and video art into a single breathtaking experience under the Roman sky.

The facade of the Costanzi “pulses” to the notes of celebrated opera arias, performed live by three singers from the Fabbrica Young Artist Program of the Teatro dell'Opera di Roma: soprano Erika Beretti singing “Vissi d'arte” from Puccini's Tosca, soprano Roberta Mantegna with “Casta diva” from Bellini's Norma, and mezzosoprano Reut Ventorero performing “Una voce poco fa” from Rossini's Il barbiere di Siviglia. The video also features the extraordinary participation of Alessandra Amato, étoile of the Theatre's Corps de Ballet.

The Vision La Visione La Vision

The concept transforms the austere neoclassical architecture of the Costanzi into a living canvas. The 3D videomapping wraps around columns, windows, and cornices, respecting every architectural detail while reinventing it through light. The building breathes, its surfaces dissolving into operatic worlds — from Tosca's dramatic silhouette against radiating light beams to the ethereal blue of Norma's moonlit temple.

An open-air enchantment of music and art. An initiative that pays homage to opera and anticipates the wonders staged inside the most sumptuous theatre of the Capital. Projections of operas colour the facade and render its austere architecture magnificent with forms and colours.

Behind the Scenes Curiosità Coulisses

  • The three singers — Erika Beretti, Roberta Mantegna, and Reut Ventorero — were all members of the Fabbrica Young Artist Program, the prestigious training programme of the Teatro dell'Opera di Roma, bringing the next generation of opera talent to the streets of the city. Le tre cantanti — Erika Beretti, Roberta Mantegna e Reut Ventorero — facevano tutte parte del programma Fabbrica Young Artist Program, il prestigioso programma di formazione del Teatro dell'Opera di Roma, portando la nuova generazione del talento lirico nelle strade della città. Les trois chanteuses — Erika Beretti, Roberta Mantegna et Reut Ventorero — faisaient toutes partie du Fabbrica Young Artist Program, le prestigieux programme de formation du Teatro dell'Opera di Roma, portant la nouvelle génération du talent lyrique dans les rues de la ville.
  • Alessandra Amato, étoile of the Teatro dell'Opera di Roma's Corps de Ballet, brought an extraordinary physicality to the projections — her dance sequences, filmed and then mapped onto the building, transformed the rigid architecture into a living, breathing body in motion. Alessandra Amato, étoile del Corpo di Ballo del Teatro dell'Opera di Roma, ha portato una fisicità straordinaria alle proiezioni — le sue sequenze di danza, filmate e poi mappate sull'edificio, hanno trasformato la rigida architettura in un corpo vivente in movimento. Alessandra Amato, étoile du Corps de Ballet du Teatro dell'Opera di Roma, a apporté une physicalité extraordinaire aux projections — ses séquences de danse, filmées puis mappées sur le bâtiment, ont transformé l'architecture rigide en un corps vivant en mouvement.
  • The 3D mapping technology required a precise survey of the Costanzi facade, including every column, window recess, and cornice. The projections were calibrated to create anamorphic illusions — windows that appear to open, columns that dissolve, walls that seem to breathe with the music. La tecnologia di mapping 3D ha richiesto un rilievo preciso della facciata del Costanzi, comprese ogni colonna, rientranza delle finestre e cornice. Le proiezioni sono state calibrate per creare illusioni anamorfiche — finestre che sembrano aprirsi, colonne che si dissolvono, muri che sembrano respirare con la musica. La technologie de mapping 3D a nécessité un relevé précis de la façade du Costanzi, incluant chaque colonne, renfoncement de fenêtre et corniche. Les projections ont été calibrées pour créer des illusions anamorphiques — des fenêtres qui semblent s'ouvrir, des colonnes qui se dissolvent, des murs qui semblent respirer avec la musique.
