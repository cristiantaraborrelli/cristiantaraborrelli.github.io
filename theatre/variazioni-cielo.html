Variazioni sul Cielo

Inspired by Margherita Hack · Mittelfest / Teatro Rossetti

Production
Teatro Stabile del Friuli-Venezia Giulia
Promo Music

Role
Virtual Space Design

Direction & Video
Fabio Massimo Iaquone

Year
2004

A performance for lights, sounds and dreams. Variazioni sul Cielo is a multimedia theatre piece freely inspired by the book Sette variazioni sul cielo by the great Italian astrophysicist Margherita Hack — a journey through spectroscopic and dreamlike images where science and dreams, brought together, create a universe in which it is still possible to live.

Cristian Taraborrelli designed the virtual space — a luminous translucent cube that becomes planet, womb, and observatory — the scenic device through which body, light, and projection merge into a single cosmic organism. Alongside Fabio Massimo Iaquone's video creations and Sandra Cavallini's performance, the space becomes a living constellation: the performer inhabits the projections, the projections inhabit the performer.

Cristian Taraborrelli ha progettato lo spazio virtuale — un cubo luminoso traslucido che diventa pianeta, grembo e osservatorio — il dispositivo scenico attraverso cui corpo, luce e proiezione si fondono in un unico organismo cosmico. Insieme alle creazioni video di Fabio Massimo Iaquone e alla performance di Sandra Cavallini, lo spazio diventa una costellazione vivente: la performer abita le proiezioni, le proiezioni abitano la performer.

☆ Margherita Hack (1922–2013) ☆ Margherita Hack (1922–2013) ☆ Margherita Hack (1922–2013)

The first Italian woman to direct an astronomical observatory (Trieste), Margherita Hack was one of the most influential astrophysicists of the twentieth century and an extraordinary science communicator. Her book Sette variazioni sul cielo — seven meditations on the cosmos, from the ancient Babylonians to modern spectroscopy — became the poetic and scientific backbone of this performance.

Hack participated directly in the project, lending her voice and image to the video sequences that punctuate the seven chapters. Her presence on the screen — lucid, ironic, passionate — transforms the performance into a genuine encounter between art and science, between the rigour of research and the freedom of dreams.

This performance is dedicated to her memory and to all those who look up at the sky with wonder.

The Vision La Visione La Vision

The scenic concept revolves around a single, luminous element: a translucent cube that floats on the stage like a celestial body. Designed by Cristian Taraborrelli, this virtual space is simultaneously screen, container, and character — a three-dimensional projection surface onto which Iaquone's video creations paint constellations, spectrograms, and human bodies in weightless flight.

The performer, Sandra Cavallini, moves in dialogue with this architecture of light. She enters the cube, is engulfed by projections, becomes part of the cosmos. The seven chapters — from prologue to epilogue — unfold as a celestial cartography: each variation is a new sky, a new frequency, a new way of seeing the universe through Margherita Hack's luminous gaze.

Behind the Scenes Curiosità Coulisses

  • The translucent cube — Taraborrelli's central scenic invention — was built with semi-transparent fabric stretched over a modular frame, allowing projections to wrap around the performer from all directions, creating a true three-dimensional holographic effect years before such technology became mainstream. Il cubo traslucido — invenzione scenica centrale di Taraborrelli — fu costruito con tessuto semi-trasparente teso su una struttura modulare, permettendo alle proiezioni di avvolgere la performer da tutte le direzioni, creando un vero effetto olografico tridimensionale anni prima che tale tecnologia diventasse di uso comune. Le cube translucide — invention scénique centrale de Taraborrelli — fut construit avec un tissu semi-transparent tendu sur une structure modulaire, permettant aux projections d'envelopper la performeuse de toutes les directions, créant un véritable effet holographique tridimensionnel des années avant que cette technologie ne devienne courante.
  • The performance premiered at Mittelfest 2004, the prestigious Central European festival in Cividale del Friuli, before touring to Teatro Rossetti in Trieste and other venues across Italy, including GiovedìScienza in Turin. Lo spettacolo ha debuttato al Mittelfest 2004, il prestigioso festival dell'Europa centrale a Cividale del Friuli, per poi essere rappresentato al Teatro Rossetti di Trieste e in altri teatri italiani, tra cui Giovedì Scienza a Torino. Le spectacle a été créé au Mittelfest 2004, le prestigieux festival d'Europe centrale à Cividale del Friuli, avant de tourner au Teatro Rossetti de Trieste et dans d'autres lieux en Italie, dont GiovedìScienza à Turin.
  • Margherita Hack, who passed away in 2013, was the first Italian woman to direct an astronomical observatory. Her direct participation in this project — lending her voice, her image, and her scientific vision — makes Variazioni sul Cielo one of the rare performances where a world-class scientist becomes an integral part of a theatrical creation. Margherita Hack, scomparsa nel 2013, fu la prima donna italiana a dirigere un osservatorio astronomico. La sua partecipazione diretta a questo progetto — prestando la sua voce, la sua immagine e la sua visione scientifica — rende Variazioni sul Cielo uno dei rari spettacoli in cui una scienziata di livello mondiale diventa parte integrante di una creazione teatrale. Margherita Hack, disparue en 2013, fut la première femme italienne à diriger un observatoire astronomique. Sa participation directe à ce projet — prêtant sa voix, son image et sa vision scientifique — fait de Variazioni sul Cielo l'un des rares spectacles où une scientifique de renommée mondiale devient partie intégrante d'une création théâtrale.
  • The collaboration between Taraborrelli and Iaquone on this project prefigured their later work together on Dom Juan with Giorgio Barberio Corsetti and on Planetaria — a creative partnership spanning two decades at the intersection of scenic design and video art. La collaborazione tra Taraborrelli e Iaquone in questo progetto prefigurava i loro lavori successivi insieme su Dom Juan con Giorgio Barberio Corsetti e su Planetaria — un sodalizio creativo che attraversa due decenni all'intersezione tra scenografia e video arte. La collaboration entre Taraborrelli et Iaquone sur ce projet préfigurait leurs travaux ultérieurs ensemble sur Dom Juan avec Giorgio Barberio Corsetti et sur Planetaria — un partenariat créatif couvrant deux décennies à l'intersection de la scénographie et de l'art vidéo.
